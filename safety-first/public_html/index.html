Types of Waste

Recycling

Recyclable trash refers to waste materials that can be collected, processed, and manufactured into new products. This includes materials such as paper, cardboard, glass, metal, and certain types of plastic. By recycling recyclable materials, we can conserve natural resources, reduce energy consumption, and reduce the amount of waste sent to landfills. The process of recycling involves collecting and sorting recyclable materials, then processing them into new products.

Compost

Compost refers to organic waste materials that are capable of breaking down and decomposing into compost through natural processes. Examples of compostable trash include food waste, yard waste, and biodegradable plastics. These materials can be collected and processed in a composting facility, where they are decomposed by microorganisms, resulting in a nutrient-rich soil amendment.

Trash

Trash refers to discarded waste materials, such as food scraps, paper, glass, plastic, or other unwanted items that are no longer needed or used. Trash is typically generated by households, commercial businesses, and industrial processes, and it is usually collected and transported to a waste processing facility or landfill for disposal. The composition of trash can vary depending on a number of factors, including local waste management practices, cultural and economic factors, and changing patterns of consumption and production.
