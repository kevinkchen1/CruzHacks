Tips for Identifying Disposable Items

Trash refers to any waste material or discarded item that is no longer useful and considered to be unwanted. This typically includes items such as paper, plastic, glass, food waste, and other similar items that are generated from daily activities and need to be disposed of. It is usually separated into different categories based on its composition and potential for recycling or harm to the environment.


Compostable materials are biodegradable items that can break down into natural elements. Compostable items include food waste, such as fruits and vegetables, ard waste, such as leaves, grass clippings, branches and paper products, such as napkins and paper towels.


Recyclable items are materials that can be reused to make new products after they are no longer needed. This includes items like paper, cardboard, plastic bottles, metal cans, and glass containers. To determine what is recyclable in your area, check with your local recycling program or waste management service.
